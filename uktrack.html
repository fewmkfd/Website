The British Grand Prix is closely associated with Formula 1. Being the longest-standing race on the racing calendar, Silverstone was the venue for the inaugural Formula 1 World Championship race in 1950 and is widely recognized as the epicenter of British motor racing.

British GP 2024

The British Grand Prix, the most seasoned race on the F1 World Championship schedule, is one of only two events that have been consistently held in every season of the championship, the other being the Italian Grand Prix. Having its inaugural race in 1926, the British GP has been an annual fixture since 1948 and served as the host for the first championship race in 1950. Currently taking place at the Silverstone Circuit, the 2020 edition marked the 70th occurrence of the race as a World Championship event and the 53rd time it was held at Silverstone. Between 1950 and 1977, the British GP was occasionally designated as the European Grand Prix, an honorary title bestowed each year upon a race in Europe.

History

The British Grand Prix, one of the oldest and most prestigious events in Formula 1 history, has been a cornerstone of the sport since its inception in 1950. Initially held at the Silverstone Circuit, this iconic race has witnessed countless memorable moments and rivalries that have left an indelible mark on motorsport. Legends like Sir Stirling Moss, Jim Clark, and Lewis Hamilton have all tasted victory on this hallowed track, solidifying their places in racing history. Silverstone's high-speed straights and challenging corners have provided a testing ground for both drivers and their machines, making the British Grand Prix a beloved fixture on the F1 calendar and a favorite among fans worldwide. Over the years, it has evolved and adapted, showcasing the relentless pursuit of excellence in Formula 1 racing while continuing to captivate audiences with thrilling performances.
